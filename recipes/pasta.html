Giulio's Pasta

A humble bowl of bean pasta.

Overview

This is a rustic, one-pot Italian-inspired dish that turns humble pantry staples into something silky, savoury and deeply comforting. Red kidney beans lend body and creaminess to the sauce, while the pasta cooks right in the same pot.

Best enjoyed with a drizzle of olive oil and freshly cracked pepper on top.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil, to coat the pot
  • 1 clove garlic, core removed
  • 1 can red kidney beans (include the liquid)
  • 2.5 cans of water (adjust for desired consistency)
  • A small palmful of salt
  • Broken spaghetti pasta

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a medium pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the garlic clove and cook until lightly browned, then remove it.
  3. Pour the entire contents of the red kidney bean can into the pot and stir well to combine with the oil.
  4. Add water a cup at a time, stirring as you go and bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Once boiling, add the salt and stir until dissolved.
  6. Add the pasta and set a timer for two minutes less than the package cooking time.
  7. Continue stirring periodically as the pasta cooks and thickens the broth.
  8. If there's too much liquid, ladle out a small amount and set aside.
  9. When the timer rings, remove from heat and stir again to reach a creamy consistency.
  10. Serve warm, optionally topped with olive oil or grated cheese.
